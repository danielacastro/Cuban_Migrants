The Route

The map below shows the route the Cubans took to get to the U.S, facing many risks along the journey like coyotes, threats from corrupt law enforcement officers, and wrecked boats to sail choppy waters.

Cubans who left the island went to Ecuador because they were not required to have a visa, but since the humanitarian crisis in Costa Rica exploded, the Ecuadorian government started to request tourist visas for Cubans, on December 1, 2015, as a way to curb the massive flow of Cubans.

Cuba and Ecuador have good diplomatic relations.
